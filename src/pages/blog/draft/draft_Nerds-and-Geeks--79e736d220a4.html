Nerds and Geeks.

Growing up, words like these were thrown around too often. In high school, I was a tall white skinny kid who took honors classes and played trumpet in every band on campus. High school kids were expected to say stupid things to each other. They weren’t always directed towards me, but I heard geek, band geek, nerd, dork and more. Unfortunately, there was always an underlining, negative context

I’m not too far past those days, but nerd has taken a new meaning. I proudly call myself a nerd. I study software engineering, intern as a web developer in Silicon Valley, read books about technology, enjoy learning new programming languages, and play with Arduino boards. I’m nerdier than I ever was in high school. 

In today’s tech-driven world, words like “nerd” and “geek” need to take on new definitions. 

Nerd means your smart.

There were so many nerds in my high school and I respected each and every one of them. They were experts at what they did. We had sports nerds who could rattle off the stats of every modern baseball player. We had automotive nerds who spent their weekends rebuilding engines. We had music nerds who discussed the fundamentals of classic music compositions.

Nerd means your passionate.

The kids who were stereotyped were interested in the “boring” things. The things like theater, math, electronics, or writing. But the students talking about which computer processor is better are not far off from the skaters showing off their why their favorite boards are the best. 

Recently, I overheard a conversation between three men on a bus. They got to the point of almost screaming at each other because they couldn’t agree on who had the best burger. It wasn’t a simple conversation, as they got into texture, bun type, ingredients, grade of meat, preparation, seasoning, and execution. These guys were serious food nerds and a detailed, passionate conversation could’ve happened with any group of nerds talking about any subject. 

Nerd means you’ve found where you excel.

Success comes from finding the overlap between your passions and in demand skills. 

Nerd has nothing to do with how you dress. 

“The difference between nerds and geeks, is that nerds get laid.” Maybe it’s not the perfect way to describe it, but it makes sense. Geek is a subculture the same way that bodybuilders are part of a subculture.

Always be nerdy about something. Be geeky if you want.
