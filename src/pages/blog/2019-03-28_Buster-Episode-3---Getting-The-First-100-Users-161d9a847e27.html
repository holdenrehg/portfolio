Buster Episode 3 — Getting The First 100 Users

Slowly But Surely

I’ve written this before, but starting a business is not easy.

We’re a few months into 2019. It’s still so damn difficult.

So far I’ve technically created 3 different products for the business.

  1. A free Slack app with 1000+ users.
  2. A “coming soon” PAAS product.
  3. A newly released product for small businesses called Buster.

And I’m finally making money. Users signing up for Buster is the first revenue collected for the business and the LLC was formed about 2 years ago. I’m not sure many people think “I’m going to start a business and we’ll make our first dollar in a couple of years!”

But that’s what happened. And it’s still under 100 users but I’m proud of what’s been done.

To be fair, every business is a little bit different.

  • Maybe I’m not running the business the way that I’m supposed to.
  • Maybe I should be 100% all-in on the business.
  • Maybe I should be running around trying to raise a bullshit funding round.
  • Maybe I’m not “hustling” enough.

But I’m doing it my way, a realistic way, and enjoying the stuff I work on. I’m devoting a lot of time to it trying to build something high quality. I’m completely bootstrapping the development, marketing, content, and design so that I’m spending less than $50 a month right now.

I feel like I’ve started to climb a pretty big hill, but at least have a little bit of momentum behind me.

It seems backward.

Building the stuff isn’t exactly easy. It can be lonely. It can be stressful. But overall I still think it was pretty low pressure, considering everything is in your head or on your computer. You can to sit in peace at times and work at your pace, trying to build something good.

It’s like cruising downhill on a sled. You’re a little worried you might hit a tree, but it’s still a good time.

Then you launch the thing.

Now you’re trying to climb back uphill. You’re being judged on your ability to climb uphill. You’re competing against 5 other climbers. Those climbers actually have snowmobiles. And there’s a pack of wolves at the bottom of the hill.

Again. Exciting, if you’re the right type of sociopath. But there are a lot more things to juggle. All of a sudden you have to worry about trying to get people to check out the thing you built, convince them it’s worth a few bucks, explain how to use it, make and keep more promises, file taxes, etc. etc. etc.

But that’s where I’m at.

All I can do is keep pushing forward. Learn from the best. Experiment. Analyze. Build. Write. Sell. And whatever allows me to keep progressing.

Hope if you’re working on a business, you’ve been able to have some success with those first 100 users too.
